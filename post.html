Interactive Storybook

The Tale of a Ballerina Told Through Processing

My storybook is aimed at a target audience of an age between 2-6, with the intention that parents read the story with their children engaging with them using technology.

When I first presented my storybook to the class there were a number of very helpful suggestions given to me to assist in the development of story. Firstly, my images were inconsistent: I had taken it upon myself to hand draw my graphics, scan them, upload them onto Photoshop and then turn them into colourful vectors. This was a lot more time consuming than I had first expected, so I had to resort to using some of the original images from the story I had taken inspiration from (from the African Storybook website). Secondly, my buttons, while not in the way of any text, should be moved and a back button should be added. Finally, there were some minor details to be changed such as playing around with different fonts and changing some of the backgrounds.

I have successfully used solely my own graphics as well as changed some of the backgrounds, as well as changed the font.

However, I did have some troubleshooting that though I painstakingly tried to correct just could not complete the task. I had managed to reposition the buttons and added a back button, however, I could not get them to function. Secondly, technology acted up, and my Photoshop software would not allow me to give all my graphics transparent backgrounds, which meant that I was not able to place different backgrounds behind them.

Overall, this assignment was my most challenging and frustrating of my entire university career. I do see the value in learning a programming language such as Java to assist in my website development knowledge, which I believe could greatly benefit my desire to begin a start-up news site in the future. So, this assignment has helped introduce me to some of the Java syntax which could benefit me in any future programming courses I decide to undertake.

Interactive Timeline

The Evolution of Taxi-Related Violence in the City of Cape Town, 1970-2016

Assignment brief: As a class, we were instructed to plot a timeline surrounding the evolution of a protest in the City of Cape Town regarding land and spatial issues, food security and agricultural protests, transport-related issues, or public facility protests.

I decided to cover the evolution of the 'taxi war' protests that have infiltrated daily news production from the 1970s to the present.

It was my initial intention to solely cover the Westlake Village community, because I live in Tokai which is virtually on the doorstep of this community. So, the protests that have been emerging within this community in the past 5 years, has affected my day-to-day living.

However, in order to gain a better understanding of the issues that have caused this protest to have such an extended lifespan, it was critical that a broader area be studied. Therefore, I have decided to include the greater Cape Peninsula area into the evolutionary process.

Overall, the protests are centered around the political ongoings of South Africa, being drastically influenced by the deregulation of the taxi industry during apartheid. In the end, this multi-billion Rand industry is one that is fraught with contestation and has endangered the public in the process.

Data Visualisation

Mapping the distribution of the Transport and Urban Development vote in the City of Cape Town

The City of Cape Town stipulated in this year’s budget, that it is seeking to become an efficient, effective and development-orientated public service, which will be empowering, and will promote fair and inclusive citizenship for every member of the public. The overall objective within the Province, is that the City of Cape Town becomes the best-run regional government in the world.

The budget is segmented into 11 votes which focus on specific areas of development within the City of Cape Town. I chose to focus on vote 11, which deals with the theme of Transport and Urban Development (TDA). This vote makes up the second largest portion of the City’s capital budget expenditure totalling R1,826,435,710. According to the Integrated Development Plan (IDP*) the TDA aligns itself with the objective of being an opportunity city and an inclusive city: the City aim to achieve this by increasing mobility by effective public transport, as well as creating integrated transport systems.

This particular budget vote progression has seen a slight decline from the previous fiscal year (2016/17), and it is estimated according the MTREF* to continue this downward progression into the next fiscal period. The allocation and usage of the budget in 2017/18, with regards to funding of the vote, is being sucked out of the energy, finance and informal settlements votes in terms of what was allocated and used in previous fiscal years.

While the Western Cape can boast in a reduction of poverty levels, the income inequality levels are still high: there are 47 wards that earn an average annual household income of between R12,400 to R29,400 per year. In terms of earning, these wards would be considered to make up the lower income brackets within society. It would be expected that a significant portion of the City’s budget would be allocated towards the upliftment of impoverished areas, with the City even claiming that the newly released 2017/18 budget is pro-poor.

In order to draw a conclusion regarding the City’s pro-poor claim, an investigation surrounding the allocation of the TDA budget per ward, in conjunction with the annual household income per ward, was taken upon. The investigation revealed that out of the 47 wards with the lowest annual household income bracket, 37 of the wards were either allocated the lowest range (R20,000 to R80,000), or were simply not allocated any of the TDA budget at all. In fact, 33 of these wards were not allocated any portion of the TDA assigned portion of the total Capital budget. Furthermore, ward 70 whose average annual household income is that of R461,000 per annum, has been allocated R280,000 from the TDA budget. From these findings, I can conclude that the City of Cape Town’s 2017/18 budget, is not pro-poor, with regards to the Transport and Urban Development vote.

Die Stad Kaapstad, het in hierdie jaar se begroting, bepaal dat dit beoog om ‘n doeltreffende, effektiewe en ontwikkelingsgerigte staatsdiens te word, wat bemagtigend sal wees en regverdige en inklusiewe burgerskap vir elke lid van die publiek sal bevorder. Die Provinsie se oorkoepelende doelwit is, dat die Stad Kaapstad die beste bestuurde plaaslike regering in die wêreld word.

Die begroting is gesegmenteer in 11 stemme wat op spesifieke gebiede van ontwikkeling binne die Stad Kaapstad fokus. Ek het gekies om op stem 11 te fokus. Die stem handel oor die tema Vervoer en Stedelike Ontwikkeling (VSO). Hierdie stem vorm die tweede grootste gedeelte van die Stad se kapitaalbegrotingsuitgawes van R1,826,435,710. Volgens die Geïntegreerde Ontwikkelingsplan, (GOP*) stem die VSO ooreen met die doel om ‘n geleentheidstad en ‘n inklusiewe stad te wees. Die Stad beoog om mobiliteit te verhoog deur effektiewe openbare vervoer, asook om geïntegreerde vervoerstelsels te skep.

Hierdie spesifieke begrotingstemvordering het ‘n effense daling van die vorige boekjaar (2016/17) gesien, en volgens die MTIUR* word beraam dat hierdie daling sal voortduur na die volgende boekjaar. Die toekenning en gebruik van die begroting in 2017/18 ten opsigte van die befondsing van die stemming, word verkry uit die energie, finansies en informele nedersettingstemme in terme van wat toegeken en gebruik is in vorige boekjaar.

Terwyl die Wes-Kaap in ‘n vermindering van armoedevlakke kan spog, is die ongelykhede van inkomste steeds hoog: daar is 47 wyke wat ‘n gemiddelde jaarlikse huishoudelike inkomste van tussen R12,400 en R29,400 per jaar verdien. In terme van verdienste, sal hierdie wyke oorweeg word om die laer inkomste tussen die samelewing te vorm. Daar sal verwag word, dat ‘n beduidende gedeelte van die Stad se begroting toegewys sal word, vir die opheffing van arm gebiede, met die Stad wat selfs beweer dat die nuwe uitgereikte 2017/18-begroting pro-arm is.

Ten einde ‘n gevolgtrekking te maak oor die Stad se pro-arm eis, is ‘n ondersoek aangaande die toekenning van die VSO – begroting per wyk in samewerking met die jaarlikse huishoudelike inkomste per wyk geneem. Die ondersoek het aan die lig gebring, dat uit die 47 wyke met die laagste jaarlikse huishoudelike inkomstebeweging, 37 van die wyke die laagste reeks (R20,000 tot R80,000) toegewys is, of glad nie enige van die VSO – begroting toegeken is nie. Trouens, 33 van hierdie wyke is nie ‘n gedeelte van die VSO toegewysde gedeelte van die totale Kapitaalbegroting toegeken nie. Verder is wyk 70, wie se gemiddelde jaarlikse huishoudelike inkomste R461,000 per jaar is, R280,000 van die VSO – begroting toegeken. Uit hierdie bevindings, kan ek tot die gevolgtrekking kom, dat die Stad Kaapstad se 2017/18-begroting nie pro-arm is ten opsigte van die Vervoer en Stedelike Ontwikkeling stem nie.
